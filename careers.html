Medical, Dental, and Vision Benefits

Available to full and part-time employees who work a minimum of 20 hours per week from date of hire. Domestic partner coverage is available. Employees each receive a weekly Cafe plan contribution that can be used toward health plan costs. Read More

Competitive Pay

Employees are paid weekly and are eligible for annual pay increases based on their job performance. All regular full and part time employees are eligible for a bonus of up to 10% of their annual salary. Read More

401(k) Retirement Plan

To encourage and help employees to plan for their retirement, NCU offers a 401(k) plan and matches 100% dollar for dollar up to 7% of an employee's salary. We also make an annual, age-weighted discretionary contribution to employees' 401(k) plans. Read More

Vacation and Paid Leaves

We recognize the importance of vacation and person time. Employees begin with 3 weeks of vacation their first year and earn an addition week after their 5th, 10th, 15th year of service. They can carry up to two weeks vacation over to the next year. Read More

Tuition Reimbursement

NCU has a generous Tuition Reimbursement Program for employees working 20 hours or more per week and who are pursuing their Undergraduate and Graduate degrees for a variety of Business and/or Banking related majors. Read More

Student Loan Repayment

Student loans can be overwhelming. To assist employees with the repayment of eligible student loans, NCU provides student loan repayment assistance up to a lifetime maximum of $10,000 for employees working 20 hours or more hours per week. Read More

Life at NCU

NCU is unlike any place you've ever worked, and maybe even any place you've interviewed. Our employees really care about our members and each other and strive to make a positive difference in all that we do.

Live Life #THEDCUWAY
Live Life #THEDCUWAY


Norsk Credit Union, better known as NCU, works each and every day to carry out the credit union ideology of People Helping people, whether it is lending money to help a member buy their first home or lending a hand to help those in need.

[A member service representative on the phone speaks] "We are able to approve the refinance of your current loan."

NCU's vision that all members achieve their financial goals is at the forefront of everything that they do. This is one reason that they have earned the trust of over 500,000 member nationwide and have grown to become the largest credit union in New England.

NCU works to make a meaningful and positive difference in the lives of members. That's just the NCU way.

[A member service representative on the phone speaks] "Thank you for banking with NCU."

Three simple philosophies make up the NCU Way: People Come First; Do the Right Thing; Make a Difference.

NCU's commitment to making a difference extends beyond banking and into the communities where its members live and work. This is done through financial education, community event sponsorships and volunteer support.

Recognizing the growing importance to support other community organizations that make a difference, NCU incorporated "NCU for Kids" in 2005. Since that time, NCU for Kids has made charitable contributions to deserving causes and charities that strive to make a difference in the lives of children and families. From assisting in after-school programs to providing comfort for a child in a hospital, NCU is proud to give support where it is needed most.

[Cam Neely speaks:] "NCU has been extremely important to the Cam Neely Foundation for Cancer Care. Not only the monetary support but by way of them introducing the foundation to more people certainly gives us an opportunity to provide support, comfort and hope to other cancer patients and families. Just any time we can get a partnership like we have with NCU, and have had for many years, it certainly made a big difference in what the organization and my foundation can do.

[Fran Hurley, President, Boys & Girls Clubs of Metro West speaks:] "NCU with the foundation NCU for Kids is a great member of the community, and they not only support us, but they support many other worthwhile programs. Their foundation is always looking for ways to help in the community."

[Gary Violette, Director, Boys & Girls Club of Hudson speaks:] "NCU is a very important partner for the Boys & Girls Club. Without partners like NCU, we wouldn't be able to run these programs."

Beginning in 2005, NCU for Kids began a partnership with the Boomer Esiason Foundation. And through a variety of efforts has raised funds to heighten awareness, education, and the quality of life for those affected by cystic fibrosis, while providing support to research aimed at finding a cure.

[Boomer Esiason speaks: ] "I started the Boomer Esiason Foundation back in 1993 when my son, Gunnar, was diagnosed with cystic fibrosis. And at the time, people with the disease weren't expected to live past their teens. So the last thing I was going to do was to sit back and count the days. That's just wasn't going to happen. That's not my nature. And in 2005, like we've done in many years prior, we always look for people to partner with, and we began a partnership with NCU and their charitable foundation NCU for Kids. Now through a variety of efforts, from our side and NCU, we've raised funds to heighten awareness and education, and also extend the quality of life for those afflicted by cystic fibrosis, all the while providing support to research aimed at finding a cure.

[Boomer Esiason speaks: ] "Now the Boomer Esiason Foundation exists to inspire and support those with cystic fibrosis and we have one great purpose and our ultimate goal is pretty simple: it's to free the world of cystic fibrosis. Now in the meantime while we improve the lives of those with the disease, NCU understands the significance of this through their commitment to our foundation, the Boomer Esiason Foundation.

[Boomer Esiason speaks: ] Now those with cystic fibrosis are now living well into their thirties and forties and even some well beyond. They are having babies of their own. They are getting married. These are all the dreams I have for my son, Gunnar. But I also know that there is a long, long way to go before we can cure this disease. And thankfully NCU does care.

[Boomer Esiason speaks: ] Now I can personally attest to that. Their culture is parallel to my foundation's culture. Together I know that we are never going to quit. And we are determined to finish this task as we fulfill our destiny. Thank you for everyone at NCU for supporting the Boomer Esiason Foundation!"

The NCU Way means supporting Boys and Girls Clubs throughout New England, providing assistance to veterans returning from serving over seas, assuring the local food banks and pantries are able to feed the hungry, advancing research opportunities at hospitals, donating to causes that are helping families whose loved ones are coping with cancer, cystic fibrosis or autism. That's making a difference, the NCU Way!

Career Growth

We want you to be at NCU for the long haul and provide all the tools so that you can be successful. You grow your skills, career, friends, and network all at NCU.

Tuition Reimbursement

NCU will advance tuition for both Graduate and Undergraduate studies for up to two courses each semester.

Undergraduate Work

All regular full-time (35-40 hours/week) and part-time (20-34.75 hours/week) employees are eligible for undergraduate tuition assistance at a regionally or nationally accredited college or university after completing six months of continuous employment.

NCU will reimburse tuition costs up to $800/course, up to five courses per year for regular full-time employees and three courses per year for regular part-time employees working a minimum of 20 hours/week.

Graduate Work

All regular full-time (35-40 hours/week) and part-time (20-34.75 hours/week) employees are eligible for graduate tuition assistance at a regionally or nationally accredited college or university after completing one year of continuous employment.

NCU will reimburse tuition costs up to $1,250/course, up to five courses per year for regular full-time employees and three courses per year for regular part-time employees working a minimum of 20 hours/week.

Training & Mentorship

Mentorship

NCU sets our employees up to be successful through a culture that promotes helping, supporting and enabling others to be the best they can be. 

We take a keen interest in the growth of employees interested in staying at the credit union, but potentially changing roles. Vice Presidents frequently coordinate shadow days and on-the-job learning opportunities in the departments employees aspire to.

Career Development Counseling

We assist employees in identifying and improving skills so that they may expand and enrich their current job or change careers. This is accomplished through internal on-the-job training to improve job skills, internal cross-training, educational aid for continuing education or seminars, and our job-posting program.

Ongoing Training Programs

Training in NCU's products, procedures, sales skills, and systems is essential for staff to perform efficiently and satisfy members. We provide our employees with a number of one-time and on-going training programs, including new hire training, product training modules, sales training, diversity training, computer skills, system training and leadership development.

Annual Innovation Contest

NCU hosts an annual Innovation Contest where our employees can present their ideas to the Senior Team. Employees are challenged to provide ideas on cutting-edge banking and financial innovations. Cash prizes are awarded to winning teams. The best ideas are implemented.
