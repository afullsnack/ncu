NCU Mobile App

Download the NCU Mobile App to your Android™ phone, iPhone®, or iPad® through the Apple App Store or Google Play, and you can conduct most banking transactions from anywhere and at any time, without visiting a branch.

Everyday Banking Features

  • Check balances and view recent transactions on your deposit and loan accounts.
  • Transfer money instantly and securely between your NCU deposit and loan accounts.
  • Transfer money to other NCU members or pay another person using People Pay.
  • Add a biller, such as your utility company, and setup a one-time or recurring bill payment.
  • Setup deposits to your NCU accounts from other institutions with Payment Center.
  • Setup payment to NCU loans from accounts at other institutions with Payment Center.
  • Deposit checks and get the funds the same day, if submitted by standard processing time.
  • Set a savings goal and create recurring transfers to help you achieve it.
  • Locate the nearest NCU branch/ATM, CO-OP branch, or surcharge-free ATM.
  • Check your inbox for important messages and alerts from NCU.
  • Setup and manage your online and mobile banking text alerts.
  • Check your monthly FICO® score and learn about the factors that impact your credit.
  • Update your personal information, including your phone number and email address.
  • Conduct a balance transfer from an outside credit card to a NCU Platinum Credit Card.
  • Manage Visa® card settings, such as blocking transactions and freezing cards.
  • Setup travel notifications to let us know when you'll be traveling outside of the country.
  • Apply for a new consumer loan or credit card, with easy, pre-filled applications.
  • Accept any preapproved consumer loan or credit card offers using Loan Suite.
  • Activate our Earn More feature on eligible accounts and check your enrollment status.
  • Obtain a loan payoff amount letter on a chosen date, using our Payoff Calculator.
  • Upload and manage documents that you need to securely share with NCU.
  • Visualize your spending and budgets easily, and simply using Money Management.

Information Security

  • For user authentication and reporting purposes, member numbers are stored but never shared or visible to outside parties.
  • Mobile Banking data is encrypted using the Wireless Transport Layer Security (WTLS) protocol and HTTPS/SSL, which provides the highest level of security.
  • A session timeout will happen after 20 minutes of inactivity which prevents someone from picking up your phone and accessing accounts.

Browser Settings

While a variety of browsers may work to use Online Banking, only the latest versions of the following browsers are supported to work as intended. Click a browser name to expand and see how to ensure you have the right browser settings.

Google Chrome

How To Accept 3rd Party Cookies: 

  1. Open the Settings page by clicking on the icon with the three stacked dots to the right of the address bar.
  2. Select Settings from the drop-down menu.
  3. Select on the left-hand menu Privacy and Security. Select Site Settings.
  4. Under Permissions, select Cookies and site data.
  5. Within Cookies and site data, “Allow sites to save and read cookie data” should be enabled (toggled in the ON position).
  6. “Block third-party cookies” should be disabled (toggled in the OFF position).
    • Recommended: “Clear cookies and site data when you quit Chrome” should be disabled (toggle in the OFF position). If this is enabled, MFA will be required at each login, even if the computer was enrolled as “Private”. 

How To Delete All Temp. Cookies and Internet Files: 

  1. Open the Settings page by clicking on the icon with the three stacked dots to the right of the address bar.
  2. Select Settings from the drop-down menu.
  3. Select on the left-hand menu Privacy and Security. Select Clear browsing history.
  4. Set Time range to “All time”
  5. Check off “Cookies and other site data” for cookies and “Cached images and files” for cached data.      
  6. Click Clear Data.

How To Set Recommended Security Settings: 

  1. Open the Settings page by clicking on the icon with the three stacked dots to the right of the address bar.
  2. Select Settings from the drop-down menu.
  3. Select on the left-hand menu Privacy and Security. Select Site Settings.
  4. Under Permissions, select JavaScript.
  5. JavaScript should be enabled (toggle in the ON position). 
    1. Recommended: .Click the (<--) arrow next to JavaScript to return to previous page.
    2. Under Permissions, select Pop-ups and redirects.
    3. Pop up and redirects should be enabled (toggled in the ON position).
    • It is recommended that "Pop-up windows" be set to Allow because Internet Banking utilizes pop-up windows (e.g. Printer Friendly window, etc.).   

*Please note that Google Chrome no longer supports NPAPI plugins. This includes Java. Java is required to utilize direct scanning with NCU’s Online Deposit service. Because of this, Online Deposit for Google Chrome will only function with the “Zero Client” version of the service.

Microsoft Internet Explorer

How To Accept 3rd Party Cookies: 

  1. Access the Tools menu by clicking on the gear icon in the upper right-hand corner next to the URL bar. 
  2. Select Internet Options from the drop-down menu.
  3. A pop-up box will display. Choose the Privacy tab. Under the Settings header, click on Advanced.
  4. Ensure First-party Cookies and Third-party Cookies are both set to “Accept”.
  5. Ensure “Always allow session cookies” is enabled (checked).
  6. Click “OK”.

How To Delete All Temp. Cookies and Internet Files: 

  1. Access the Tools menu by clicking on the gear icon in the upper right-hand corner next to the URL bar. 
  2. Select Internet Options from the drop-down menu.
  3. A pop-up box will display. Choose the General tab (default). Under the Browsing history header, click Delete.
  4. Check off “Cookies and website data” for cookies and “Temporary Internet files and website files” for cached data. Click “Delete”.
    • Recommended: In the General tab under Browsing history “Delete browsing history on exit” should be disabled (unchecked). If this is enabled, MFA will be required at each login, even if the computer was enrolled as “Private”.

How To Set Recommended Security Settings: 

  1. Access the Tools menu by clicking on the gear icon in the upper right-hand corner next to the URL bar. 
  2. Select Internet Options from the drop-down menu.
  3. A pop-up box will display. Choose the Security tab.
  4. Set the “Security level for this zone” slider to Medium (selecting “default level” will allow for slider to display if it is not already)

OR

  1. Click the button "Custom Level"
  2. Scroll to Downloads (near middle of list). “Under File Download”, make sure the radio button is set to Enable.
  3. Scroll to Scripting. Under “Scripting of Java applets” (near bottom of list), make sure that the radio button is set to Enable.
  4. Click “OK”.

AND (after either method of level settings)

  1. Click the Advanced tab.
  2. Under Security (last header in the list), ensure "do not save encrypted pages to disk" is disabled (unchecked).
  3. Under the Security header, ensure "Use SSL 3.0" AND "Use TLS 1.0" is enabled (checked).
  4. Click “OK”.
